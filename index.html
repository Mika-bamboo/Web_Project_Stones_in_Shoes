Sandals let stones land on your foot easily — but they also fall right back out. Low-cut sneakers are the real trap. The collar gap is wide enough for stones to enter during toe-off, but once inside, the enclosed upper keeps them trapped against your foot.

Boots block entry entirely. The tall shaft and scree collar seal the opening — confirming this is a problem footwear designers already know about.

The "worst" shoe isn't the most open one — it's the one that traps stones most effectively.
