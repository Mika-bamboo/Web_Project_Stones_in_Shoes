Stone-In-Shoe — UI wireframe

Scroll-driven storytelling structure for the interactive physics simulation. Six acts, progressively revealing controls and insights.

Act 1 — The hook
Auto-playing walking animation
No controls. The user simply watches a person walk. After a few steps, a counter pops up revealing stones have entered the shoe. Goal: spark curiosity.

Design note: Zero interactivity here. Pure observation. The popup counter is the only surprise element — it appears after ~5 steps to break the passive watching.

Act 3 — Zoom in: the entry point
Collar gap cross-section + collar controls
Camera shifts to the ankle area. A cross-section view shows the collar gap opening and closing during the gait cycle. Stone particles hover near the edge. User controls collar height and heel notch width.
